================================================================================
emAIl Sentinel — Icon & Banner Prompts for Nano Banana (or any AI image generator)
================================================================================

Generate three images for a Google Workspace Marketplace listing for an
app called "emAIl Sentinel." The app is a Gmail add-on that monitors your
inbox with AI and sends alerts via email, SMS, Google Chat, Calendar,
Sheets, and Tasks.

IMPORTANT CONSTRAINTS (Google Marketplace requirements):
  - Icons MUST be square
  - Icons MUST have a fully transparent background (PNG with alpha)
  - Icons MUST be in color (not grayscale)
  - Icons MUST NOT contain any Google logos, Gmail icons, or Google
    brand colors (blue/red/yellow/green quad-color)
  - Icons MUST NOT contain any text or letters
  - Keep the design simple enough to be recognizable at 32x32 pixels


================================================================================
IMAGE 1 — App Icon (128x128 pixels)
================================================================================

Prompt:

A clean, modern app icon on a transparent background. The design is a
notification bell with a small envelope tucked behind it or overlapping
at the lower-left. The bell is ringing (small motion lines or a subtle
glow at the top). Use a bold, saturated primary color — deep electric
blue (#2563EB) or vibrant teal (#0891B2) — for the bell. The envelope
is a slightly lighter or complementary shade. The style is flat/semi-flat
with subtle shadows (like Material Design 3 icons). No text, no letters,
no Google logos. Minimal detail — this must read clearly when shrunk to
32 pixels. Transparent background, PNG format, 128x128 pixels.

Alt prompt (if the bell+envelope doesn't look right):

A modern flat-design shield icon on a transparent background. The shield
is a bold teal or electric blue. Inside the shield is a simple white
envelope silhouette with a small alert/exclamation indicator at the
top-right corner of the envelope. Clean lines, no text, no gradients
heavier than a subtle top-to-bottom fade. Transparent background, PNG,
128x128 pixels.

================================================================================
STYLE NOTES
================================================================================

Brand color palette (use these consistently):
  Primary:    #2563EB (electric blue) or #0891B2 (teal)
  Accent:     #F59E0B (amber — for the alert/notification indicator)
  Dark:       #1E40AF (deep blue — for gradients and backgrounds)
  Light:      #FFFFFF (white — for icon silhouettes on dark backgrounds)

Style:
  - Flat / semi-flat (Material Design 3 inspired)
  - Not 3D, not skeuomorphic, not hand-drawn
  - Not overly detailed — must work at tiny sizes
  - Professional SaaS feel, not consumer/playful

What to avoid:
  - Google's brand colors used together (the blue/red/yellow/green combo)
  - Gmail's envelope icon (looks too much like a Google trademark)
  - Any text or lettering inside the icon itself
  - Busy backgrounds or textures
  - Rounded-rectangle app-icon frames (Android/iOS style) — Google's
    Marketplace does its own framing

================================================================================
AFTER GENERATION
================================================================================

1. Verify the icons have actual transparency (not a white background
   that looks transparent). Open in Preview/Photoshop/Figma and confirm
   the checkerboard pattern shows through.

2. If the generator produced slightly off-size images, resize in Canva:
     128x128, 32x32, 220x140 — exact pixel dimensions.

3. Export as PNG-24 with alpha channel.

4. To get the lh3.googleusercontent.com URL required for appsscript.json:
   - Upload the 128px icon to Google Drive
   - Right-click → Share → "Anyone with the link" → Copy link
   - Extract the file ID from the URL
   - Your manifest logoUrl becomes:
     https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/d/YOUR_FILE_ID_HERE

5. Update appsscript.json logoUrl with the real URL.

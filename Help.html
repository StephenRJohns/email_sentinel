emAIl Sentinel™ watches your Gmail for new messages and sends an alert when one matches a rule you describe in plain English. Rules are evaluated by Google Gemini.

Plans & pricing
Free includes up to 3 rules, automatic polling every 3 hours or slower, Google Calendar / Sheets / Tasks alerts, SMS alerts (any provider — 6 presets plus generic webhook), and the standard activity log (ring buffer of the most recent ~60 entries, retained for 30 days).
Pro ($4.99/month or $39/year) adds unlimited rules, automatic polling as fast as every 1 hour (3× faster than Free), Google Chat webhook alerts, MCP server alerts (Slack, Teams, Asana, custom), AI-assisted rule writing, and unlimited log history.
Founding-member lifetime ($79 one-time) is available to the first 500 purchasers only — permanently retired after 500 sold.
Upgrade from the home card. Downgrading preserves your rules but disables Chat / MCP channels and clamps polling back to the Free 3-hour minimum.

Why polling is hourly, not by-the-minute
Google enforces a 1-hour minimum on time-based triggers in every Google Workspace add-on. The Free (3-hour) vs. Pro (1-hour) split is our tier policy on top of that platform floor.

We could technically poll your Gmail faster by running our own backend server — that's how some products work — but that would require:
• Storing your Gmail OAuth refresh tokens on our servers
• Routing your email content through our infrastructure
• Reading and evaluating each email outside your own Google account

We deliberately don't. emAIl Sentinel runs entirely as a Google Workspace add-on inside your own Google account. Your email content never reaches our servers — we never see, store, or process it. The only places your email data goes are: (1) the Google Gemini API, called with your own API key, to evaluate rules and format alerts, and (2) the alert channels you configure (your SMS, your Calendar, your Sheets, etc.). The 1-hour polling floor is the price of that architecture, and we think it's worth paying. Use Scan email now for on-demand checks whenever you need them.

Manual checks anytime
Whatever your polling interval, you can click Scan email now on the home card (or pick it from the universal "⋮" menu) to run an immediate check. This is the way to verify a new rule, confirm a fresh email triggers an alert, or just bypass the polling cadence on demand. Background polling continues normally afterward.

Quick start
1. Open Settings and paste your Gemini API key. Get one free at aistudio.google.com/app/apikey.
2. (Optional) Configure SMS — pick a provider in Settings (click SMS setup guide for a comparison), then add named SMS recipients below the provider fields.
3. (Optional) Add Google Chat spaces, MCP servers, or calendar/sheet/task IDs in Settings to target them from rules.
4. Open Rules and click + New rule, or click Starter rules on the home card to create 5 pre-built rules (urgent emails, invoices, shipping updates, security alerts, and subscription renewals). Starter rules are created disabled — edit each one to tick alert channels and enable it.
5. Back on the home card, click Start monitoring. A time-driven trigger will run in the background even when Gmail is closed.

Writing a rule
Rules are plain English. Be specific about senders, subjects, attachments, or body keywords. Examples:
• "Any email from anyone @example.com that has a PDF attachment that looks like an invoice or purchase order."
• "Subject contains URGENT or CRITICAL."
• "Email from boss@example.com asking for a status update."

Each rule has an Alert message content field — plain-English instructions that tell Gemini how to format the alert that recipients receive. The default includes date, sender, subject, summary, and any action items. Click Help me write the rule text or Help me write the alert text in the rule editor to have Gemini draft a starting point for you.

Rule examples by alert channel
Every rule works with every channel — just enable the channels you want in the rule editor. Here are real-world examples showing which channels make sense for each use case.

SMS alerts — best for urgent, time-sensitive notifications
• Server down alert: Rule: "Any automated email about a server outage, service degradation, or critical alert from our monitoring system." Alert format: "One line: service name, severity, and what's affected." Channels: SMS to the on-call engineer
• Wire transfer notification: Rule: "Email from the bank confirming a wire transfer, ACH payment, or large transaction over $10,000." Alert format: "Amount, sender/recipient, and date only." Channels: SMS to the CFO
• Security alert: Rule: "Any email about a failed login attempt, password reset request, or suspicious activity on any of our accounts." Alert format: "Which account, what happened, and when." Channels: SMS to yourself

Google Chat alerts — best for team-visible notifications
• Sales lead: Rule: "Any email from a new contact (not in our company domain) that mentions pricing, demo, trial, or buying." Alert format: "Company name, contact name, what they're interested in, and their email." Channels: Chat to "Sales Leads" space
• Support escalation: Rule: "Any email with subject containing ESCALATION, P1, or CRITICAL from a customer." Alert format: "Customer name, issue summary, and severity." Channels: Chat to "Support Escalations" space
• Shipping notification: Rule: "Email from FedEx, UPS, USPS, or DHL with a tracking number or delivery confirmation." Alert format: "Carrier, tracking number, and expected delivery date." Channels: Chat to "Office Operations" space

Google Calendar alerts — best for time-based follow-ups and phone notifications
• Meeting request: Rule: "Any email asking me to schedule a meeting, call, or demo." Alert format: "Who's asking, what they want to meet about, and any suggested times." Channels: Calendar event (you'll get a phone notification)
• Deadline reminder: Rule: "Any email that mentions a deadline, due date, or 'by end of day/week'." Alert format: "What's due, when, and who's asking." Channels: Calendar event (so it shows on your calendar)
• Travel itinerary: Rule: "Emails from airlines, hotels, or booking services with a confirmation or itinerary." Alert format: "Confirmation number, dates, and location." Channels: Calendar event + Sheets log

Google Sheets alerts — best for audit trails, reporting, and team visibility
• Compliance log: Rule: "Any email from a regulatory body, auditor, or containing the words 'compliance', 'audit', or 'regulation'." Alert format: "Date, sender, subject, and a one-sentence summary." Channels: Sheets log (creates a searchable compliance record)
• Expense tracking: Rule: "Emails containing receipts, invoices, or payment confirmations." Alert format: "Vendor, amount, date, and category." Channels: Sheets log
• Hiring pipeline: Rule: "Any email from job candidates or recruiting platforms." Alert format: "Candidate name, position applied for, and current stage." Channels: Sheets log + Chat to "Hiring" space

Google Tasks alerts — best for to-do items that need follow-up
• Action item catcher: Rule: "Any email that explicitly asks me to do something, review something, or approve something." Alert format: "What's being asked, by whom, and any deadline." Channels: Task (appears in your Gmail sidebar as a to-do)
• Contract review: Rule: "Email with an attachment that looks like a contract, NDA, agreement, or legal document." Alert format: "From whom, document type, and any stated deadline." Channels: Task
• Follow-up needed: Rule: "Any email where someone says 'let me know', 'please confirm', 'get back to me', or 'awaiting your response'." Alert format: "Who's waiting, what they need, and when they sent it." Channels: Task (so you don't forget to reply)

Combining multiple channels
The real power is mixing channels on a single rule:
• Critical vendor issue: Rule: "Email from any @example.com address marked urgent or mentioning an outage." Channels: SMS (instant phone buzz) + Chat "Client Escalations" space (team sees it) + Calendar event (blocks your time) + Sheets (audit trail)
• New hire onboarding: Rule: "Email from HR containing 'new hire', 'onboarding', or 'start date'." Channels: Task (add to your to-do list) + Sheets (track all new hires) + Chat "People Ops" space

Labels (vs. folders)
Gmail uses labels rather than folders. Use INBOX for the inbox, or any label name as it appears in Gmail. Labels containing spaces are fine — type them exactly as shown. Multiple labels: comma-separated.

Alert channels
SMS — Google doesn't ship an SMS API. emAIl Sentinel works with any SMS provider you want — six have quick-start presets built in; anything else goes through the Generic webhook. Click SMS setup guide in Settings for a comparison of the presets with sign-up links. After picking a provider, add named SMS recipients (e.g. "On-call", "CFO") in the Settings SMS recipients section so rules can pick them by name instead of raw phone numbers. Quick overview of the presets:
• Textbelt — easiest: 1 free SMS/day with API key "textbelt", no sign-up needed
• ClickSend — free trial, just username + API key, no phone number needed
• Vonage — free trial credits, no credit card required
• Telnyx — cheapest per message at scale, requires a rented phone number
• Plivo — $10 free credit, requires a rented phone number
• Twilio — most popular, best docs, $15 free credit, requires a rented phone number
• Generic webhook — POST to any HTTPS endpoint you control; use this for any SMS provider without a built-in preset
Current per-SMS and phone-number prices are shown in the SMS setup card in Settings and on each provider's website. Prices change — verify before committing.
After configuring, click Send test SMS to verify it works.

Google-native alert channels (free)
These use your existing Google account — no third-party sign-up, no cost:
• Google Chat — post to a Chat Space via webhook, just like Teams. Requires a Google Workspace paid account (webhooks are not available on free Gmail accounts). To set one up:
  1. Go to chat.google.com and create a Space (or use an existing one)
  2. Open the space, then click the space name in the top header bar → Apps & integrations → Webhooks
  3. Click Add webhook, give it a name (e.g. "emAIl Sentinel"), click Save
  4. In Settings → Google alert channels, enter the space name in "Chat space N name" and paste the webhook URL in "Chat space N webhook URL"
  5. Select the space name in each rule where you want Chat alerts
• Google Calendar — creates a 15-minute calendar event with the alert details. Your phone will notify you if calendar notifications are on. Enable per rule with a checkbox.
• Google Sheets — appends a row to a spreadsheet (auto-created on first alert if you don't specify one). Great for an audit trail. Enable per rule with a checkbox.
• Google Tasks — creates a task in Google Tasks with the alert details. Shows in Gmail sidebar and the Tasks app. Enable per rule with a checkbox.

MCP server alerts
Send alerts to Slack, Microsoft 365 / Teams, Asana, or any custom HTTP endpoint that speaks the Model Context Protocol (JSON-RPC 2.0 over HTTPS). In Settings → MCP server alerts, click Add MCP server, pick a type (load defaults fills the tool name and args template), paste the endpoint URL and auth token, and save. Then tick the server in any rule to fire that tool when the rule matches.

Gemini pricing and model tiers
emAIl Sentinel calls the Gemini API twice per evaluated email per rule: once to check if the email matches the rule, and once to format the alert message. Understanding the cost tiers helps you pick the right model and avoid unexpected quota hits.

Models (select in Settings)
• Gemini 2.5 Flash (default) — fast, highly capable, best balance of speed and quality. Recommended for most users.
• Gemini 2.5 Flash Lite — ultra-low-cost variant; slightly less capable but cheapest on the paid tier. Good for simple rules at very high volume.
• Gemini 2.5 Pro — highest accuracy for complex or nuanced rules, but higher cost. Best reserved for demanding rules where precision matters.
• Gemini 2.0 Flash 001 — stable versioned release of the previous-generation Flash model; a fallback if 2.5 Flash behaviour changes.

Free tier (no billing required)
A key from aistudio.google.com/app/apikey is free — no credit card needed. Flash models allow 1,500 requests/day and 1,000,000 tokens/day, resetting at midnight Pacific Time. When you hit the limit, Gemini returns a 429 error and emAIl Sentinel logs "Gemini quota exceeded" in the Activity Log. Calls resume automatically the next day — you are never charged on a free key.

How many calls will you use per day?
Each new email in a watched label triggers one Gemini evaluation per active rule. Already-seen messages are skipped entirely.
Estimate: new emails/day × active rules × 2 = daily API calls
• 20 new emails/day × 1 rule = 40 calls — well within free
• 50 new emails/day × 3 rules = 300 calls — well within free
• 100 new emails/day × 5 rules ≈ 1,000 calls — approaching free limit; consider billing
• 200 new emails/day × 10 rules = 4,000 calls — needs paid tier

Paid usage
If you regularly hit the free limit, enable billing at aistudio.google.com → your API key → Enable billing. The same API key continues to work — no change in emAIl Sentinel settings is needed. Approximate paid rates (verify current prices at ai.google.dev/pricing):
• 2.5 Flash / 2.0 Flash 001: ~$0.075/million input tokens, ~$0.30/million output tokens
• 2.5 Flash Lite: ~$0.04/million input tokens, ~$0.15/million output tokens
• 2.5 Pro: ~$1.25/million input tokens, ~$5.00/million output tokens
At Flash rates, a typical user (50 emails/day, 3 rules) costs under $1/month. Even a power user (100 emails/day, 5 rules) typically stays under $3/month.

Tips to minimize Gemini usage
• Enable Business hours — skips checks outside your configured time window (supports overnight windows too)
• Watch specific labels (e.g. Vendors/Invoices) instead of INBOX — only emails in that label are evaluated
• Combine related conditions into one rule instead of creating two separate rules for the same email type
• Keep your Alert message content prompt concise — shorter instructions produce shorter (cheaper) output

Business hours
Optionally restrict checks to a daily window. Outside of business hours the trigger still fires but skips the actual mail check, so you don't burn Gemini quota overnight.

Polling
Polling is selected from a whole-hour dropdown in Settings. Free plan options: 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, or 24 hours. Pro plan options: the same plus 1 hour and 2 hours at the top. The 1-hour limit is a Google Workspace add-on platform limit: time-based triggers in any Workspace add-on cannot fire more often than once per hour. We can't go faster — it's a Google constraint, not a tier policy. The Free / Pro 3-hour vs. 1-hour difference is a tier policy on top of that platform floor.

To run a check sooner than the next scheduled fire, click Scan email now on the home card — that runs immediately regardless of cadence and is available on every plan. The first run for each label baselines existing messages so you don't get an alert flood when you first install — alerts start with the next new message.

Reset baseline
The first time the Service checks a watched label, it records every existing message ID as a "seen" baseline so you don't get a flood of alerts on install — alerts only fire for mail that arrives after that point. Clicking Reset baseline in Settings deletes that stored set. On the next run, every label is treated as brand-new: existing messages are silently absorbed into a fresh baseline, and alerting resumes for mail that arrives after. Use it if alerts start firing for old mail (e.g. after changing labels or reinstalling), or any time you want a clean slate.

Privacy
Settings, rules, the seen-message baseline, and the activity log are all stored in UserProperties, which is private to your own Google account. Email contents are sent only to Google's Gemini API and to the alert channels you configure (SMS provider, Chat webhooks, MCP server endpoints) — never anywhere else.

Troubleshooting
• "No Gemini API key configured" — open Settings and paste a key, then click Test Gemini.
• "Label '…' fetch failed" — verify the label exists in Gmail with that exact name (case-insensitive).
• SMS not delivered — open the Activity Log to see the provider's error response.
• Alerts firing for old mail after install — open Settings and click Reset baseline; the next run will re-baseline.
• Back arrow (←) disappeared from the sidebar — every card (Rules, Settings, Help, Activity Log, Starter Rules) includes a Home button at the top. Tap it to return to the home card any time the Gmail navigation arrow is not shown.
• Lost edits in the rule or settings editor — always click Save before tapping the back arrow. Google's add-on framework gives no event when the system back arrow is pressed, so the editor cannot prompt to save unsaved changes. Each editor card shows an amber notice at the top as a reminder.
• MCP target (Asana / Slack / Teams) not populated and no error in the activity log — the dispatcher now parses Streamable-HTTP text/event-stream responses and surfaces tool-level errors as MCP alert to "<name>" FAILED: MCP "<name>" tool error: <detail>. Common details: Project not found (bad project_id), Forbidden (PAT lacks workspace access), Required field missing. The auth header must be Bearer <token> with a literal "Bearer " prefix.
• Activity log or Sheets row dates look off by several hours — dates use your primary Google Calendar's timezone. Fix it in calendar.google.com ▸ Settings ▸ Time zone, then run a fresh check.
• Still stuck? Open a GitHub issue — issues are tracked, searchable, and get the fastest response.

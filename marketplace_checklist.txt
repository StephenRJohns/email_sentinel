PART 1 — LISTING ASSETS YOU NEED TO CREATE
================================================================================

These are required fields in the Google Cloud Console → Google Workspace
Marketplace SDK → Store Listing page.


1. APPLICATION NAME
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
   Max 50 characters. Title case. No version numbers. No "Google" or
   Google product names. Must match the OAuth consent screen app name
   exactly.

   Recommended:  MailAlert

   (Short, title-case, no Google trademarks, under 50 chars.)


2. SHORT DESCRIPTION
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
   Max 200 characters. Appears in search results and the app card.

   Recommended:

     "AI-powered email monitoring for Gmail. Write rules in plain
     English — Gemini evaluates every new message and alerts you
     via email, Chat, Calendar, Sheets, Tasks, or SMS."

   (198 characters. Hits all the key search terms.)


3. DETAILED DESCRIPTION
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
   Max 16,000 characters. Appears on the listing page below screenshots.
   This is your sales copy and your biggest SEO surface.

   Recommended (customize as needed):

     MailAlert watches your Gmail for new messages and sends alerts
     when one matches a rule you describe in plain English. Rules
     are evaluated by Google Gemini — no regex, no code.

     ALERT CHANNELS (mix and match per rule):
     • Email — sent from your own Gmail, no SMTP server needed
     • Google Chat ��� post to any Chat Space via webhook
     • Google Calendar — creates an event with phone/desktop notification
     • Google Sheets — appends a row for audit trails and searchable history
     • Google Tasks — creates a task in your Gmail sidebar
     • SMS — via Twilio, Telnyx, Plivo, Textbelt, ClickSend, Vonage, or
       any custom webhook

     HOW IT WORKS:
     A background trigger checks Gmail every 1-30 minutes (you choose),
     even when your browser is closed. For each new message in a watched
     label, Gemini reads the sender, subject, body, and attachment names
     and decides whether it matches your rule. If yes, it formats an
     alert message and fires every channel you configured.

     EXAMPLE RULES:
     • "Any email from tma.com with a PDF that looks like an invoice."
     • "Subject contains URGENT or CRITICAL."
     • "An email from boss@company.com asking for a status update."

     PRIVACY:
     Everything stays in your Google account. Rules, settings, and alert
     history are stored in per-user UserProperties — private to you.
     Email content is sent only to Google Gemini for evaluation and to
     whichever alert channels you enable. Nothing else.

     SETUP:
     1. Install the add-on.
     2. Paste a free Gemini API key (aistudio.google.com/app/apikey).
     3. Create a rule.
     4. Click Start Monitoring.

   (Adjust tone/length to taste. Include keywords people search for:
   "gmail alert", "email notification", "email monitoring", "gemini",
   "ai email filter", "sms alert", "google chat notification".)


4. CATEGORY
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
   Choose one from Google's list. Best fit:

     "Productivity" or "Communication"

5. ICONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
   Google requires multiple sizes. All must be:
   - Square
   - Colored (not grayscale)
   - Transparent background (PNG)
   - No Google trademarks or brand assets

   Required sizes:
     128 x 128 px  (main listing icon)
      32 x 32 px   (small icon)

   What to put on it:
     A notification bell, an envelope with a bell, or a shield with a
     mail icon. Use your brand color. Keep it simple — it will be tiny
     at 32px.

   Tools:
     - Canva (canva.com) — free, has icon templates at exact pixel sizes
     - Figma (figma.com) — free, precise pixel control
     - DALL-E or Gemini image generation — generate concepts, then clean
       up in Canva/Figma

   IMPORTANT: After creating the icon, you must host it at a public URL
   that starts with https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/ for the manifest
   logoUrl field. The easiest way:
     1. Upload the 128px PNG to Google Drive
     2. Share it publicly ("Anyone with the link")
     3. Get the file ID from the share URL
     4. The lh3 URL is:
        https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/d/<FILE_ID>
     5. Replace the logoUrl in appsscript.json with this URL


6. CARD BANNER
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
   220 x 140 pixels. Appears on the marketplace card.
   Use the same visual language as the icon. Include the app name.


7. SCREENSHOTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
   Minimum 1, maximum 5. Size: 1280 x 800 pixels.
   Square corners, no padding. Must accurately show real app functionality.

   Recommended screenshots (in order):
     1. Home card — showing monitoring status, rule count, Start button
     2. Rules list — showing 2-3 rules with status, labels, channels
     3. Rule editor — showing a filled-in rule with all fields
     4. Settings card — showing Gemini config and SMS provider dropdown
     5. Activity log — showing a successful match and alert dispatch

   How to take them:
     1. Deploy the add-on and configure it with real rules
     2. Open Gmail → click the MailAlert icon in the right rail
     3. Take a full-page screenshot of the browser (the add-on panel
        will be on the right)
     4. Crop to 1280x800 focusing on the add-on panel
     5. Or use Chrome DevTools → device toolbar to set a fixed viewport

PART 2 — GOOGLE CLOUD CONSOLE CONFIGURATION
================================================================================

Before submitting, configure these in the Google Cloud Console for the
project linked to your Apps Script:


11. OAUTH CONSENT SCREEN
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
    Google Cloud Console → APIs & Services → OAuth consent screen

    - User type: External (for public listing)
    - Publishing status: In Production (NOT Testing)
    - App name: "MailAlert" (must exactly match appsscript.json name)
    - User support email: your email
    - Developer contact: your email
    - App logo: upload your 128px icon
    - Authorized domains: your domain (if hosting legal docs there)
    - Scopes: add all scopes from appsscript.json

    IMPORTANT: If your consent screen is still in "Testing" status,
    Google will reject your listing.


12. ENABLE THE MARKETPLACE SDK
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
    Google Cloud Console → APIs & Services → Library

    Search for "Google Workspace Marketplace SDK" → Enable it.
    Then go to the SDK configuration page and fill in the store listing.


13. OAUTH VERIFICATION
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
    Because MailAlert uses sensitive scopes (gmail.modify, gmail.send,
    gmail.readonly), you must submit for OAuth verification:

    Google Cloud Console → APIs & Services → OAuth consent screen →
    Verification status

    Google may require:
    - A video walkthrough of how the add-on uses each scope
    - A written justification for each scope
    - A link to your privacy policy

    Scope justifications for MailAlert:

      gmail.addons.execute    Required to run as a Gmail add-on.
      gmail.modify            Required to search and read messages in
                              user-selected labels for rule evaluation.
      gmail.send              Required to send alert emails from the
                              user's Gmail account when a rule matches.
      gmail.readonly          Required to read message content (sender,
                              subject, body, attachments) for AI
                              evaluation against user-defined rules.
      gmail.labels            Required to list Gmail labels so users can
                              select which labels to monitor.
      calendar                Required to create calendar events as
                              alerts when users enable this channel.
      spreadsheets            Required to log alert data to Google
                              Sheets when users enable this channel.
      tasks                   Required to create Google Tasks as alerts
                              when users enable this channel.
      script.external_request Required to call the Google Gemini API
                              for rule evaluation, and to call
                              third-party SMS providers (Twilio, etc.)
                              when users enable SMS alerts.
      script.scriptapp        Required to create and manage time-driven
                              triggers for background email monitoring.

    If Google requires a third-party security assessment:
    - Cost: $2,000-$15,000
    - Timeline: 2-6 weeks
    - Approved assessors: https://support.google.com/cloud/answer/9110914

PART 4 — SEO (MARKETPLACE SEARCH OPTIMIZATION)
================================================================================

The Marketplace has its own search algorithm. Here's how to rank well:


19. KEYWORD STRATEGY
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
    People search for these terms in the Marketplace. Include them
    naturally in your short description, detailed description, and
    (where appropriate) your app name:

    Primary keywords (high intent):
      gmail alert, email alert, email notification, email monitoring,
      gmail add-on, email watcher

    Secondary keywords (feature-specific):
      ai email filter, gemini email, smart email alert,
      sms email notification, google chat alert,
      email to calendar, email to sheets, email to tasks

    Long-tail keywords (specific use cases):
      alert when email arrives, notify me of important emails,
      email rule plain english, ai email rule, email trigger alert

    Where to use them:
      - Short description: 1-2 primary keywords
      - Detailed description: all of them, naturally woven into the text
      - DO NOT keyword-stuff. Google penalizes this.
      - The detailed description I wrote in section 3 above already
        includes most of these naturally.


20. SCREENSHOTS MATTER FOR CONVERSION
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
    Screenshots are the first thing users see after the title. They
    directly affect install rate (which affects ranking).

    Tips:
      - First screenshot should show the clearest value proposition
        (a rule matching and alert firing)
      - Use real content in screenshots, not "Lorem ipsum"
      - Consider adding brief captions/annotations to screenshots
        (but don't violate the "must accurately represent app" rule)


21. REVIEWS AND RATINGS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
    Google factors ratings into search ranking. After launch:
      - Ask early users to leave a review
      - Respond to every review (shows active maintenance)
      - Fix issues reported in reviews promptly
      - A 4.5+ star rating significantly improves discoverability
